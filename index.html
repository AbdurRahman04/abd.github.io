About Me

I am Abdur Rahman, a Master of Science student in Applied Statistics at King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM), Dhahran, Saudi Arabia. My thesis focuses on Geospatial and Machine Learning Mortality Modeling of MENA Countries.

I completed my Bachelor of Science in Statistics from The Islamia University of Bahawalpur, Pakistan. I am broadly interested in mortality forecasting, geospatial methods, and the integration of machine learning techniques in demography and public health.

My current work combines traditional demographic models with modern machine learning and geostatistical methods, with the goal of building reliable and interpretable tools for decision makers in health and social policy.

Research

Current Thesis

Geospatial and Machine Learning Mortality Modeling of MENA Countries
My thesis aims to improve mortality modelling and forecasting for countries in the MENA region by integrating:

  • Classical demographic models such as Leeâ€“Carter and related frameworks;
  • Modern machine learning methods for pattern extraction and residual modeling;
  • Geospatial techniques to capture regional heterogeneity and spatial dependence.

The project focuses on building hybrid residual learners that leverage both the interpretability of classical models and the flexibility of machine learning, while accounting for spatial structure and data quality issues common in demographic data.

Methodological and Applied Interests

  • Designing control charts for monitoring demographic indicators such as fertility and infant mortality.
  • Using the Generalized Extreme Value (GEV) distribution to detect structural changes in health indicators.
  • Hybrid deep learning architectures for forecasting time series in environmental and demographic contexts.
  • Uncertainty quantification and early warning tools for public health surveillance.
